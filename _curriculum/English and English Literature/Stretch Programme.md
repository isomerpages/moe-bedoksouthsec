### BDS Debate Team

The BDS Debating Programme is a literacy programme that provides students with opportunities to develop intellectual rigour and critical thinking in their use of the English Language. We aim to develop in our students life-long values, skills and competencies that help them acquire global awareness and develop their communication, collaborative and critical thinking skills.

The programme aims to: 

(i)  Develop **independent, confident** and **effective** users of the language through varied activities that help to hone their communication skills,

(ii)  Inculcate an **intellectual curiosity** of real-world global and social issues, and be **innovative** problem-solvers in approaching such issues

(iii) **Collaborate** as a team through active participation in various competitions, both on local and international platforms, such as the Singapore Secondary Schools Debating Championships and the World Scholars' Cup.

![BDS Debate Team](/images/BDS%20Debate%20Team.jpg)

### Singapore Secondary Schools Debating Championships (SSSDC) – Division III

This year, the debate team participated in the Singapore Secondary Schools Debating Championships (SSSDC). The competition functions as the Nationals of the debating culture in Singapore, and gives the students a chance to participate in a competition format that allows them to gauge where they stand against their peers. The competition also serves as a learning experience for the students, as they get to interact with debate adjudicators, and gather feedback personally on how to improve their debating skills. In both 2017, 2018 and 2019, the school has also hosted one of the preliminary rounds, giving our students a chance to run the rounds in the debating tournament.


This year, the team has done us proud and gotten to the semi-finals of the competition.

### World Scholar's Cup 

Since 2017, the BDS debaters have participated in the World Scholar's Cup, and have done the school proud with their achievements. The World Scholar's Cup is an academic programme that aims to celebrate the joy of learning with a global community of young scholars and future leaders. Each year, a central theme that aims to reflect the world is unveiled and this year's theme is 'An Entangled World'. Teams are then challenged to put their understanding of six subjects (Social Studies, Science and Technology, History, Arts and Music, Literature and Special Area) as they are tested in debate, writing, general knowledge, and critical analysis.


In 2017, our students qualified for the global round in Hanoi, Vietnam, which had close about 3600 participants from 40 countries, following which, they then qualified for the Tournament of Champions at Yale University, USA in November 2018. This year the BDS Debate Team participated in the global round of the World Scholar's Cup which took place in Beijing, China in June. Using the knowledge attained from the provided readings and their own research of the topics, our school team applied what they had learnt in the various competition events – Team Debate, Collaborative Writing, Scholar’s Challenge and Scholar’s Bowl. The team is proud to have done well in the various team and individual events, garnering a collection of individual and team awards, both gold and silver.

For more information about this programme, please refer to the website: [http://scholarscup.org/](https://scholarscup.org/)

#### National Schools Literature Festival


Our Literature students participated in the annual National Schools’ Literature Festival (NSLF) this year. The NSLF serves as a platform for Literature students to interact and to share ideas through debates, performances and other friendly competitions. This is all with the aim of motivating students to enjoy and celebrate Literature. This year, our school sent a total of 4 teams to participate in the various competitions such as Choral Reading, Book Parade, Unseen Poetry Debate and Set Text Debate. We are also proud to announce that our Sec 3 Literature students won their debates in the 2 debate competitions.
